Soil Swell Factors & Weight-Volume Conversion Metrics

Excavating undisturbed earth alters its structural density. Managing material logistics requires a strict understanding of Bank, Loose, and Compacted states.

The Three Material States

Earthmoving math changes depending on where the material physically sits. Missing these calculations causes massive haul-truck scheduling failures and project budget deficits:

  • Bank Cubic Yards (BCY): Material in its natural, undisturbed state before excavation.
  • Loose Cubic Yards (LCY): Material that has been dug up, creating voids and increasing total volume.
  • Compacted Cubic Yards (CCY): Material squeezed tight by field compaction gear after placement.

Volumetric Load Factors Table

Use these baseline multipliers to track volume changes when moving common earth profiles from bank to loose conditions:

Material Type Load Factor (Multiplication Metric) Average Swell Percentage
Clean Common Earth 0.80 25% Swell
Hard Clay Formations 0.70 43% Swell
Broken Blast Rock 0.60 67% Swell

Payload Warning

Never size your haul fleets using Bank dimensions. Haul wagons carry Loose states, while pay items are strictly measured in Bank or Compacted states.
