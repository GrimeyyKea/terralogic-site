Understanding Earthwork Volume States

In heavy civil operations, dirt is never just dirt. Soil changes its total volume significantly depending on whether it is sitting untouched in the ground, piled up in a haul truck, or rolled over and compressed by a heavy compactor.

The Three Main States:

  • Bank Cubic Yards (BCY): Earth in its natural state before any excavation occurs. Engineering plans utilize BCY values for primary mass-haul balances.
  • Loose Cubic Yards (LCY): Earth that has been dug up. Air voids enter the material causing it to "swell" in size during truck loading.
  • Compacted Cubic Yards (CCY): Earth that has been placed and compressed by machinery, forcing air out and making it denser than its original state.
