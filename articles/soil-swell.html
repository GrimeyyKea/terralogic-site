Soil Swell & Volume Conversion

In heavy civil earthwork, managing transitions between Bank (in-situ), Loose, and Compacted conditions directly determines haul logistics efficiency and structural accuracy.

Excavation introduces air pockets, increasing material volume (Swell). Mechanical compaction forces air out, reducing it below its original state (Shrinkage).

Material Factor Classifications

Standard conversions for volume tracking across different material profiles:

Material Type Swell Factor (Bank to Loose) Shrinkage Factor (Bank to Compacted) Load Factor (Typical)
Common Earth / Topsoil 1.25 (25%) 0.85 (-15%) 0.80
Heavy Structural Clay 1.35 (35%) 0.90 (-10%) 0.74
Sand & Fine Gravel 1.12 (12%) 0.88 (-12%) 0.89
Blasted Rock / Fragmented 1.50 (50%) 1.30 (+30% Growth) 0.67

Core Equations

Loose Cubic Yards (LCY) = Bank Cubic Yards (BCY) × (1 + Swell %)
Compacted Cubic Yards (CCY) = Bank Cubic Yards (BCY) × (1 - Shrinkage %)
