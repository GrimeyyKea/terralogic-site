Grade Control Systems

3D GPS Machine Control: Field Calibration & Tolerances

Eliminating vertical drift and holding subgrade tolerances requires tight base station placement, clean satellite geometry, and rigorous localization rules.

Localization & Control Point Tolerances

Before firing up automated hydraulics, the site localization model must be tied into physical benchmarks. Never map a project model using fewer than four high-order horizontal and vertical control points circling the perimeter.

Operation Phase Target Vertical Tolerance Target Horizontal Tolerance Recommended Base Refresh Rate
Mass Earthwork / Rough Grade ± 0.10 feet ± 0.10 feet 1 Hz
Fine Grading / Subbase ± 0.02 feet ± 0.04 feet 2 Hz or Higher

Preventing Vertical Drift in the Field

If your cutting edge starts cutting deep or riding high unexpectedly, check your satellite availability metrics. Multipath interference caused by working too close to dense timber lines or heavy structures will corrupt your vertical accuracy long before horizontal positioning drops out entirely.

Base Station Rule

Always anchor your local base station transmitter on an unyielding, surveyed benchmark away from heavy haul roads to prevent vibration drift.
