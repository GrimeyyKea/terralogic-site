Utility Systems

Gravity Pipe & Pressure Bedding Mechanics

Burying utility pipe runs is a battle against dead loads and live traffic impacts. If a trench is dug incorrectly or backfilled with poor material, the structural weight of the earth above will crush the line, causing catastrophic structural joint failure or massive ground settling.

Preventing system failure requires managing two critical vectors in the field: Minimum Cover Depth to buffer dynamic live loads (like a fully loaded haul truck passing overhead) and Bedding Class Selection to evenly distribute static soil pressure around the pipe envelope.

Rigid vs. Flexible Pipe Behavior

The type of pipe material completely dictates how structural loads are distributed down in the trench:

  • Rigid Pipe (Reinforced Concrete Pipe - RCP): The pipe inherently carries the vast majority of the load, relying on its brute cross-sectional strength. If the bottom support isn't uniform, the pipe bells will crack under pressure.
  • Flexible Pipe (HDPE / PVC): The pipe shifts and flexes under load, transferring the weight outwards into the surrounding trench walls. Because of this, the placement and density of the side-fill stone (haunching) is critical to prevent the pipe from flattening.

Standard Trench Bedding Classifications

Engineering plans dictate standard classes of structural support depending on the depth and surrounding geological stability:

Bedding Class Material Profile Bedding Factor Field Application Focus
Class A (Concrete Cradle) Poured plain or reinforced concrete arch 2.8 - 4.8 Extreme depth runs or high-impact pavement crossings
Class B (First Class) Clean crushed stone base with packed haunches 1.9 Standard mainline sanitary sewer and deep storm lines
Class C (Ordinary) Granular foundation layer with loose backfill 1.5 Shallow drainage leads, non-traffic zones

Field Geometry Specifications

To safely evaluate cover depth and manage proper trench width calculations on the fly, keep these metric rules locked down:

Minimum Cover = 12 inches (Non-Traffic) | 24 inches (Under Heavy Pavement Base)
Standard Trench Width = Outer Pipe Diameter + 16 inches (Minimum clearance for proper haunch compaction)
