Field Moisture and Proctor Balances

To build stable foundations, structural fills must reach engineered targets. Quality control inspectors utilize nuclear density gauges to measure structural soil parameters directly inside active work zones.

Why Moisture Matters:

Water acts as a mechanical lubricant during compaction. Too little moisture leaves high friction between dirt particles; too much moisture fills empty void pockets completely, preventing proper compaction because water cannot compress.

The **Proctor Curve** establishes the sweet spot, identifying the **Optimum Moisture Content** required to achieve maximum dry structural configurations.
